Marketing requires constant content creation: ads, emails, landing pages, social posts, product descriptions. AI excels at generating persuasive marketing copy when given the right prompts. This lesson teaches you how to automate marketing content while maintaining brand voice and conversion effectiveness.

Why AI for Marketing Copy?

Key Benefits:

  • Speed: Generate 10 variations in seconds vs. hours of brainstorming
  • A/B testing: Create multiple versions to test what converts
  • Consistency: Maintain brand voice across all content
  • Overcoming writer's block: Generate fresh angles and hooks
  • Scale: Personalize content for different audiences/segments

The Marketing Copy Formula

Essential Prompt Elements:

  1. Product/Service: What are you selling?
  2. Target Audience: Who is the customer? (age, profession, pain points)
  3. Key Benefit: What problem does it solve? What transformation?
  4. Tone/Voice: Professional? Casual? Urgent? Playful?
  5. Format: Email subject? Ad headline? Landing page copy?
  6. Call-to-Action: What should they do? (Buy, sign up, download)

Application 1: Email Subject Lines

Scenario: Product launch email

Prompt:

"Write 10 email subject lines for our new productivity app launching next week. Details:
- Target: Remote workers struggling with time management
- Benefit: Save 5+ hours per week with AI-powered scheduling
- Tone: Professional but friendly, urgency without being pushy
- Length: Under 50 characters for mobile
- Include: At least 3 with emojis, 3 with questions, 3 with numbers
- Avoid: Spammy words like 'Free', 'Act now', excessive punctuation"

Result: 10 testable subject lines optimized for open rates

Application 2: Facebook/Instagram Ad Copy

Effective Ad Prompt Structure:

"Create Facebook ad copy for [product]. Use this structure:

Hook (first line): Attention-grabbing statement or question
Problem: Describe the pain point
Solution: How [product] solves it
Proof: Social proof or benefit stats
CTA: Clear action step

Constraints:
- Total length: 125 characters primary text, 40-character headline
- Tone: [specify]
- Include emoji where natural
- Target audience: [describe]"

Real Example: Fitness App Ad

Prompt: "Create 3 Facebook ad variations for our fitness app. Target: Busy professionals 30-45 who want to work out at home. Key benefit: 15-minute workouts that deliver results. Tone: Motivational but realistic, no shame or guilt. Include: One focused on time savings, one on results, one on convenience. Format each with Hook, Problem, Solution, Proof, CTA."

Output: 3 complete ads ready for A/B testing

Application 3: Landing Page Copy

Landing Page Sections to Generate:

  • Hero headline + subheadline: Main value proposition
  • Problem/Solution section: Empathy + how you solve it
  • Features/Benefits: What it does + why it matters
  • Social proof: Testimonial suggestions (if you don't have real ones)
  • FAQ: Address common objections
  • CTA buttons: Multiple persuasive variations

Landing Page Prompt Template

"Write landing page copy for [product]. Structure:

1. Hero Headline (under 10 words, clear value)
2. Subheadline (1 sentence, who it's for + main benefit)
3. Problem Section (3 bullet points describing pain points)
4. Solution Section (3 paragraphs: what it is, how it works, transformation)
5. Feature-Benefit List (5 items, format: Feature name - What it means for them)
6. 3 Testimonial Templates (name, role, specific result)
7. FAQ (5 objections answered persuasively)
8. 3 CTA button variations

Tone: [specify]
Target: [audience]
Brand voice keywords: [3-5 words like 'innovative, trustworthy, bold']"

Application 4: Product Descriptions

Application 5: Social Media Posts

Platform-Specific Prompts

Platform Optimal Format Key Prompt Elements
Twitter/X 280 characters, thread option Hook first line, hashtags (2-3), conversational
LinkedIn 1300 chars, professional Value-first, industry insights, storytelling
Instagram 2200 chars, visual-focused Emojis, line breaks, hashtags (5-10), CTA in bio
Facebook 500-1000 words possible Engagement questions, community focus, longer ok

Social Media Batch Prompt

"Create a week of social media content (7 posts) for [brand]. Theme: [topic/campaign].

For each post, provide:
- Platform: [Twitter/LinkedIn/Instagram]
- Post copy (within character limits)
- Suggested visual description
- Hashtags
- Best time to post

Mix of content types: 2 educational, 2 promotional, 2 engaging/entertaining, 1 user-generated content prompt
Brand voice: [keywords]"

Maintaining Brand Voice

How to Encode Your Brand Voice:

Method 1: Voice Keywords

"Write in a tone that is: friendly, authoritative, witty, never condescending or salesy"

Method 2: Example Text

"Match the tone and style of this existing copy: [paste 2-3 examples]"

Method 3: Do's and Don'ts

"Do: Use contractions, start sentences with 'You', use analogies. Don't: Use jargon, write in passive voice, exceed 8th-grade reading level"

Copywriting Frameworks to Use in Prompts

Framework Structure Best For
AIDA Attention, Interest, Desire, Action Ads, landing pages
PAS Problem, Agitate, Solution Email campaigns, pain-point marketing
FAB Features, Advantages, Benefits Product descriptions, sales pages
4 Ps Picture, Promise, Prove, Push Long-form sales letters

Using Frameworks in Prompts

"Write an email sequence (3 emails) using the PAS framework:

Email 1: Problem (identify pain point, relate to reader)
Email 2: Agitate (make problem feel urgent, costly to ignore)
Email 3: Solution (introduce product as perfect answer, include CTA)

Product: [describe]
Audience: [describe]
Each email: 150-200 words, conversational tone, subject line included"

Brand voice: Professional but warm, empathetic, solution-focused, never pushy. Speak directly to the pain of interrupted focus.

Format each section clearly with headlines."

Key Takeaway:

Marketing copy automation is about providing detailed context: target audience, pain points, benefits, tone, format, and constraints. The more specific your prompt, the less editing required.
